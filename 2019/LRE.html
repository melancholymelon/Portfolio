3D Printed Inconel Nozzle

We are building Northeastern's first regeneratively cooled-liquid rocket engine. I, along with my colleague Jackson Stearns, have designed a Kerosene-LOX fueled liquid engine which utilizes the kerosene as both fuel and coolant.

Our injector is designed to run kerosene through the pintle and liquid oxygen through the surrounding annulus; these two flows impinge and atomize to create a mix ideal for combustion.

Pintle Injector

Nozzle with cooling channels

The kerosene will run through cooling channels situated on the jacket of both the nozzle and the chamber. The now heated fuel will then manifold at the bottom of the nozzle and be routed to the pintle injector for burn.
